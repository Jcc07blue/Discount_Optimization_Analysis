## SALES DISCOUNT OPTIMIZATION ANALYSIS

## PROBLEM STATEMENT
This project evaluates whether declining profit margins can be partially explained by inconsistent discount policies. Using SQL-based analysis, the objective is to determine whether discounts are aligned with customer value, product profitability, sales performance, and purchasing behavior, while identifying opportunities to standardize pricing decisions.

## PROJECT OVERVIEW
Industry	Retail Sales
Dataset	Sales Transactions Dataset (o el nombre real si lo tienes)
Tools	SQL Server, Excel
Business Domains	Pricing Strategy, Sales Performance, Profitability Analysis, Commercial Operations

## ANALYSIS
Six SQL queries were run to extract specific data in order to verify whether the hypothesis stated in the objective can be considered correct:
•    Average discount rate by number of units ordered: We verified whether larger orders receive greater discounts.
•    Average discount rate by total sales amount for each customer: We analyzed whether discounts are aligned with customers’ sales volume.
•    Average discount rate per sales representative: We evaluated the consistency and discretion in the application of discounts among the various sales representatives.
•    Average discount rate compared to the average margin for each product category: We checked whether the discounts applied are consistent with the actual product margins.
•    Average discount rate by year and month: We analyze the trends over time and the seasonality of the discounts applied.
•    Percentage of commission on the sales representative’s total sales compared to the average discount they apply: We examine the relationship and impact between the commissions earned by sales representatives and the discounts they choose to grant.

## KEY INSIGHTS
Client related
•    Discount on total sales:
o    Customers with similar sales volumes do not receive the same discount levels; therefore, it is evident that discounts do not increase proportionally with purchase volume.
•    Discount based on product margin:
o    The discounts granted are not proportional to the profit margin generated by each product. Higher margins are not always linked to lower discounts, which leads to inefficiency.

Seller related
•    Commission on product sales:
o    Salespeople with similar commission structures offer drastically different discounts when closing a sale. There is no clear policy governing commissions based on the discounts granted on products sold.

Product related
•    Profit margin and discount applied by product category:
o    There is a complete disconnect between each category’s profit margin and the discount applied to it. Critical cases were identified where discounts of up to 10% are applied to product categories whose profit margins are less than 10%, completely eroding profitability.

## BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES
•    Implement a standardized discount matrix: Establish an automated discount system that follows strict strategic criteria based on purchase volume, sales value, and the specific profit margin for each product and/or category.
•    Audit and align sales incentives: Continuously monitor the discounts applied by the sales force and restructure the incentive scheme, ensuring that commissions are directly proportional to sales volume and that excessive discounts are subject to contractual penalties.
